Travel is My Passion

As a Child I Dreamed about Traveling

My name is Gabriel. I was born in the Philippines but grew up in southern California. Unfortunately my mom was afraid to fly, and didn't like to travel much, so my childhoot experiences were limited to southern California. Thanks to books, movies and television as well as personal stories from friends and family, I dreamt about traveling throughout the U.S. and abroad. My first experiences traveling domestically on my own was when I attended high school in San Jose, California. I expanded my range of travel by attending college in Massachusetts which provided me with opportunities to explore New England and the mid-Atlantic states. After college I moved to the San Francisco Bay Area where I continue to travel near and far whenever I have free time and enough money.

Traveling allows me to discover for myself people and places that I heard about from personal stories, read about in books and magazines, and seen in movies and on television. For example, I wandered around Shanghai looking for what landmarks remained in the American zone that existed before World War II to get an idea of what my father may have seen when he was a little boy. I was fascinated when I saw up close what was then a divided Berlin where I observed how a wall divided a single city, and how a massive fence divided an entire country from north to south. I was happily stuffed to the gills and pleasantly drunk after eating a delicious and memorable four-course lunch with its accompanying wine pairings at a three-star Michelin restaurant run by a famous French chef in Paris.

A Journey Begins with Planning

The first step in traveling somewhere is to decide where to go. Ideas for travel destinations are often inspired by others' life stories, your own imagination and even random chance. Once you have chosen your destination, you'll need to figure out how to get there. For the sake of simplicity, let's imagine that you want to visit Barcelona, Spain. That means you'll need to do some research about flights to Barcelona and how much they cost. The easiest and simplest way to do research is to use online flight search services.

Flight Tools for Travel Planning

I like using two flight search services, Google Flights and Kayak, for researching which airlines fly to my destination and when. Flights there or back may not be available for every day of the week, may have departures only during specific times of the day or night, and may have only seasonal service which means an airline will fly to a destination only part of the year. The minimum set of information needed by a travel search engine are departure city, destination city, departure date, return date. By default most travel search engines have "economy" selected for class of service, and "roundtrip" for type of airline ticket. These two parameters normally offer the cheapest ticket prices.

How to Use a Google Flights Search Tool

I'll use Google Flights, which offers a simple user interface, to illustrate how I search for available flights and aircraft that offer the lowest ticket prices and the most comfortable seats. An example of a trip that I wish to make is a 10-day roundtrip to Barcelona, Spain where I will arrive on Saturday, September 5, 2020, and return on Monday, September 14, 2020 in the "premium economy" travel class. A basic search is outlined in the steps below:

Finding Flights to Your Destination

  1. Navigate to the Google Flights page on your browser tab .
  2. Enter the following information in the search fields listed below:
    1. Ticket type = "Round trip"
    2. Fare class = "Premium Economy"
    3. Departure city = "San Francisco", often abbreviated using its 3-letter airport code, "SFO".
    4. Destination city = "Barcelona", often abbreviated using its 3-letter airport code, "BCN".
  3. Analyze the search results to see which are the top three "Best departing flights" displayed at the top of the search results and what are the other departing flights listed below the top three flights.
    1. Find the lowest fares listed.
      The lowest fares may be for itineraries with one or more stops, very long waits at connecting airports, and for departure times that are either very early in the day or very late at night.
    2. Notice the estimated travel time for the lowest fares.
      The lowest fares may have the longest estimated travel times, i.e., the duration of time starting from the initial departure time and ending at the final arrival time. It should include time spent at any airports waiting for the connecting flight(s).
    3. Are the lowest fares for "nonstop" flights, or do they have one or more stops?
      Nonstop flights are the most desirable for most people because trips with connecting flights require hiking from one terminal gate to another at a connecting airport within the specified connection time. Catching a connecting flight on a domestic trip is simple; just walk from the arrival gate to the departure gate. International trips connecting at a foreign airport typically requires a security screening of you and your carry-on luggage, and a passport check in addition to hiking from one gate to another.
    4. Check which airport(s) are used for the connecting flight(s) if flight option has one or more stops.
    5. Click the down-arrow ("\/") link for a flight option to see its details which include departure and arrival times, total travel time, airline name, aircraft type, and ticket class.
  4. Click an outbound flight option.
  5. Click a return flight option.
  6. Analyze the selected itinerary which provides departure and return flight information as well as luggage restrictions.
    1. Check if the desired travel class, "Premium Economy", is available for each leg the the journey.
    2. Check if the aircraft listed are narrow body (single aisle) or wide body (two aisle) aircraft.
      The wide body planes may offer wider seats than narrow body planes, but not always depending upon the class of service.
    3. Check if the layover time is not too short, e.g., less than 50 mins., or too long, e.g., ten hours.
      Minimum connection/transfers times vary by airport and airline
  7. Repeat steps 1 to 6 several times for different airlines and different travel dates to get an idea if lower prices, nonstop flights, the desired travel class and more desirable departure times are available.

Some Ideas for Using Flight Data

Google Flights offers useful features like fare graphs to see if how airfares for the given destination vary up to nine to ten months in the future, and fare tracking to alert the user when the airfare is going up or down. I try to start checking airfares as soon as data is available, and buy my ticket at least three to five months before my departure date. Depending upon the destination, ticket may be sold out months before departure especially during the summer and holiday months from mid-November to early January. Ticket prices can temporarily affected by airline sales, so fare tracking alerts can notify you of possible deals which depending upon the destination may occur unexpectedly or with some frequency if your destination is serviced by lots of airlines especially low-cost airlines like Norwegian or easyJet.
